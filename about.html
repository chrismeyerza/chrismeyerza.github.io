About

A career spent building, scaling, and turning around businesses.

I'm Chris Meyer. Most recently, Group CEO of Lesaka Technologies (Nasdaq: LSAK). Before that, Joint-Managing Director of Investec Bank plc.

I've spent my career in financial services and technology — acquiring, scaling, and restructuring businesses through periods of rapid change. Along the way I've worked closely with activist investors, private equity shareholders, and management teams operating under real pressure. My focus has always been on building cultures that prioritise organisational interests over personal goals, and on translating that culture into measurable shareholder value.

What energises me most is the human side of all that — the experience of building people, teams, and businesses, and being inspired by their growth. Strategy is fun, but watching a team reach a level they didn't think they could is what makes the work matter.

What I'm doing now

I'm at MIT, deepening my understanding of how AI is reshaping product, software, and the way work gets done. I'm using the time to build small things in public — prototypes, tools, ideas in early form — partly to learn, partly to test which of those ideas have real legs.

The areas I keep coming back to are financial services, health, food, and consumer experience. They're where I think AI-assisted tools can deliver disproportionate value to people, particularly where existing solutions are clunky, generic, or built for someone else's life.

How I think about all this

I'm not a developer. I've spent most of my career a few floors removed from the people writing the code that runs the businesses I've led. What I am, increasingly, is someone who believes AI tools have made it possible for people like me — operators, executives, ex-CEOs — to actually build instead of just commission. That's a big shift, and one I think is worth taking seriously.
