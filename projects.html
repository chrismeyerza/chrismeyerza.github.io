fodmap-guru

A personalised low-FODMAP food tool for IBS sufferers. The science is well-documented — what's missing is the practical layer that maps personal taste to safe foods, then connects that to what people actually buy and eat. The current prototype is a working questionnaire that builds a personal safe/avoid list. Next step: filter Waitrose's product catalogue against the user's profile.

How I'm thinking about projects

I'm trying to keep two principles in mind as I build.

One: start with a real problem someone has, ideally one I've experienced or seen up close. The reason fodmap-guru exists is that the diet is genuinely hard to follow in real life — even with all the science freely available — and most existing tools don't address that gap.

Two: build the smallest useful version first. The instinct as an executive is to scope big. The discipline I'm practising here is the opposite: build something modest that works end-to-end, get it in front of real users, and let what they actually do shape what gets built next.
